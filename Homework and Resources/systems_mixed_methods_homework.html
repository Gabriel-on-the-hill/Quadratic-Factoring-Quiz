Part 1 — Study these examples

Example 1: Substitution
\(y = 2x - 1\) (i)
\(3x + y = 9\) (ii)
Step 1: Substitute (i) into (ii): \(3x + (2x - 1) = 9\)
Step 2: Solve: \(5x = 10 \rightarrow x = 2\)
Step 3: Find y using (i): \(y = 2(2) - 1 = 3\)
Solution: (2, 3)
Example 2: Elimination (Add/Subtract)
\(x + y = 5\) (i)
\(x - y = 1\) (ii)
Step 1: Add (i) and (ii): \((x+x) + (y-y) = 5+1 \rightarrow 2x = 6\)
Step 2: Solve: \(x = 3\)
Step 3: Find y using (i): \(3 + y = 5 \rightarrow y = 2\)
Solution: (3, 2)

Part 2 — Mixed Methods

1. Substitution (Set equal):
\(y = 3x - 4\)
\(y = 2x + 3\)
2. Substitution (Set equal):
\(y = 5x + 1\)
\(y = 7x + 13\)
3. Substitution (Variable isolated):
\(2x + y = 6\)
\(y = 4 - x\)
4. Elimination (Add/Subtract):
\(4x + y = 5\)
\(2x - y = -2\)
5. Elimination (Multiplication needed):
\(3x - 4y = 10\)
\(2x - 5y = 16\)
