Elimination Method Practice

How to Solve

We use the Elimination Method to solve a system of equations by adding or subtracting them to "eliminate" one variable.

Step Example 1: Different Signs (Add) Example 2: Same Signs (Subtract)
System $$ \begin{aligned} 2x + y &= 10 \\ 3x - y &= 5 \end{aligned} $$ $$ \begin{aligned} 4x + 2y &= 14 \\ x + 2y &= 5 \end{aligned} $$
1. Check Coefficients Look at \(y\): we have \(+y\) and \(-y\).
Signs are different, so we ADD.		 Look at \(2y\): we have \(+2y\) and \(+2y\).
Signs are same, so we SUBTRACT.
2. Eliminate $$ \begin{aligned} (2x+3x) + (y-y) &= (10+5) \\ 5x &= 15 \end{aligned} $$ $$ \begin{aligned} (4x-x) + (2y-2y) &= (14-5) \\ 3x &= 9 \end{aligned} $$
3. Solve First Variable $$ \begin{aligned} \frac{5x}{5} &= \frac{15}{5} \\ x &= 3 \end{aligned} $$ $$ \begin{aligned} \frac{3x}{3} &= \frac{9}{3} \\ x &= 3 \end{aligned} $$
4. Plug Back In Substitute \(x=3\) into Eq 1:
\(2(3) + y = 10\)		 Substitute \(x=3\) into Eq 2:
\(3 + 2y = 5\)
5. Solve Second Variable $$ \begin{aligned} 6 + y &= 10 \\ y &= 4 \end{aligned} $$ $$ \begin{aligned} 3 + 2y &= 5 \\ 2y &= 2 \\ y &= 1 \end{aligned} $$
6. Final Answer Solution: (3, 4) Solution: (3, 1)
